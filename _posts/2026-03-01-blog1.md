### ***My Journey Begins...***

#### **Who am I?**

My name is Emily Chou, and I am an international student from Taiwan currently majoring in Computer Science at SFSU. 

As someone who has experience in creating websites, I always try to focus on what layout, feature, and placement would be the best for my intended users. Whenever I come across a new website, I like to think about how this website is doing well and what it lacks design-wise. Over time, I’ve come to understand that good design is just as important as good code.
Learning about Human-Computer Interaction will help me take that mindset further. Instead of relying on instinct alone, I want to base my design decisions on research, user behavior, and proven principles. I want my projects to not only function properly but also provide the best possible experience for the people using them.


#### **What excites me aout HCI**

Before this class, I thought technology was mostly about coding and hardware. But HCI showed me that technology is not just about machines. It’s about people.
HCI focuses on how humans interact with computers, apps, websites, devices, and everything digital. It asks questions like:
- Is this easy to use?
- Does this design make people feel confident?
- Is this accessible to everyone?

What excites me most is that HCI combines psychology, creativity, design, and problem-solving. It’s not just about building something that works; it’s about building something that *works well for humans*.
And that changes everything.


#### **How HCI impacts my life**

One instance HCI has impacted my life is the calendar page on Canvas. 
As a college student, I check Canvas everyday. It can be hard to keep track of different assignments from different courses with different due dates, so I really like the calendar that Canvas has. It allows me to see all my assignments and their due dates all in one place. I think this has really helped me in keeping tract of assignmetns and help me manage my time. 
![Canvas Calendar](/assets/images/canvas.jpg)

Another instance HCI has impacted my life would be in Google Maps. 
I like how Google Maps uses colors to show how much traffic there is. 
The darker the red, the more traffic there are. This allows me to get an idea of how long a trip might be without having to enter a destination each time and checking the travel time. 
![Google Maps](/assets/images/maps.jpg)

#### **HCI and my personal growth**

Learning HCI isn’t just about becoming better at designing apps.
It’s about learning how to think differently.
HCI encourages:
- Empathy
- Observation
- Patience
- Critical thinking

Instead of asking, “Does this work?”
I want to work towards asking, “Does this work for people?”
And i think that shift in perspective is powerful.
My dream self is someone who builds meaningful tools. Someone who creates experiences that feel intuitive, inclusive, and human-centered, and I believe by learning about HCI, it will help me become that person.


#### **My project**

This semester, I will be working on a project that helps people plan their travel trips more easily.
I chose this idea because I often it always takes me a long time to plan trips, with all the different tools and tabs, the whole experience is just too overwhelming. This project is meaningful to me because I’ve experienced the frustration firsthand. I feel that if planning a trip is much easier, people would travel more often and have fewer things to worry about. 
I want to make trip planning and price comparison easier for users from all different backgrounds and age groups, and I want to make travel more accessible to everyone, so people don’t have to miss out on adventures just because they are not good with tools or planning. But more than that, I hope to grow as a designer and thinker.
