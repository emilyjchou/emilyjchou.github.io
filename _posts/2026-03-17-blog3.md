### ***Bringing Ideas to Life: Creating Your Concept Video***

### 1. From Idea to Solution: Defining Your Concept

#### What problem are you solving?

The problem I am addressing is how difficult and overwhelming it is for students to plan affordable trips. From personal experience and research, I found that users struggle with fluctuating travel prices, especially depending on the day they choose to travel. Many users end up overpaying simply because they are unaware of cheaper options.

In addition, trip planning itself requires a lot of effort — comparing flights, booking hotels, and organizing activities. This process can feel exhausting, especially for busy students who already have heavy workloads.

My concept, **TripFlow**, addresses these issues by:
- Helping users find the cheapest travel dates based on flexible inputs  
- Simplifying the trip planning process  
- Organizing all travel information in one place  

---

#### Brainstorming & Refinement

During the ideation phase, I explored several potential solutions, such as:
- A basic price comparison tool  
- A travel recommendation app  
- A centralized trip dashboard  
- An AI-generated itinerary planner  

However, I realized that many of these ideas either:
- Focused on only one part of the problem  
- Were too complex for users  
- Did not fully address user stress and time constraints  

Through refining my ideas, I decided to combine the most useful features into one solution that focuses on **simplicity and user needs**.

---

#### Why I Chose This Final Concept

I chose TripFlow because it directly addresses the core frustrations users face:
- Confusion about pricing  
- Lack of time to plan  
- Disorganization during travel  

Instead of solving just one issue, this concept provides a more complete and user-centered solution. It is simple, practical, and realistic while still being impactful.

---

#### Alignment with Blog 2 Research

From my research in Blog 2, I found that users:
- Feel overwhelmed by too many options  
- Struggle to find the best prices  
- Want a faster and easier way to plan trips  

TripFlow aligns closely with these findings by:
- Reducing decision fatigue  
- Providing clear and optimized options  
- Streamlining the entire planning process  

This ensures that the solution is not based on assumptions, but on actual user needs.

### 2. Behind the Scenes: Shooting the Concept Video

#### Planning & Filming Process

To create my concept video, I started by developing a clear storyline that shows a student struggling with trip planning and then benefiting from TripFlow.

I planned the scenes by:
- Writing a simple script  
- Breaking the story into three key tasks  
- Choosing realistic locations (desk, room, hallway)  

This helped me organize the video and ensure it clearly demonstrated the problem and solution.

---

#### Tools & Techniques Used

For this project, I used:
- My phone camera for filming  
- A laptop and props (notebook, papers) to simulate planning  
- Video editing software

![Edit](/assets/images/edit.jpg)

I also used basic techniques like:
- Close-up shots to show frustration  
- Simple transitions to show the shift from problem to solution  

---

#### Challenges & Solutions

One challenge I faced was making the problem clear without overcomplicating the video. At first, the scenes felt too long and confusing.

To solve this, I:
- Simplified the script  
- Focused on key moments instead of too many details  
- Shortened scenes to keep the video engaging  

Another challenge was filming alone, which required multiple takes and adjustments to camera angles.

![Edit](/assets/images/edit2.jpg)

---

#### Behind-the-Scenes Moments

During filming, I had several moments where I had to redo scenes multiple times, especially when trying to make reactions look natural.

I also realized that small details — like having too many tabs open or messy notes — helped better communicate the feeling of stress.

Overall, the process taught me that storytelling and clarity are just as important as the idea itself.

### 3. The User Perspective: Who Benefits from Your Solution?

#### Target Users

The primary users of TripFlow are:
- College students  
- Busy individuals with limited time  
- Inexperienced travelers  

These users often want to travel but feel overwhelmed by the planning process and unsure how to find the best deals.

---

#### How the Solution Improves Their Experience

TripFlow improves the user experience by:
- Saving time through simplified planning  
- Reducing stress by organizing all information in one place  
- Helping users make smarter financial decisions  

Instead of spending hours comparing options, users can quickly plan their trip and feel confident in their choices.

---

#### User Feedback

After sharing my concept with a few peers, the feedback was positive. Some common responses included:

- “This would save me so much time.”  
- “I always struggle with picking the cheapest dates.”  
- “I like that everything is in one place.”  

This feedback confirmed that the problem is real and that the solution is both useful and relevant to users.
